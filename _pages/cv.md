I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Industrial and Systems Engineering at MSSLAB, KAIST,
advised by Prof. Hyun-Jung Kim (Mar. 2023 – Feb. 2028, expected), supported by a
government-sponsored graduate fellowship that covers full tuition and a monthly
research stipend. I received my B.S. in Industrial and Systems Engineering from
KAIST (Mar. 2019 – Feb. 2023), with a minor in Science and Technology Policy,
where I was recognized for excellence in leadership and volunteering and held the
National Academic Excellence Scholarship in Science and Engineering — a four-year,
merit-based full scholarship awarded by the Korean government. Before KAIST, I
attended Sejong Academy of Science and Arts (Mar. 2016 – Feb. 2019), where I
received the Jang Yeongsil Award for excellence in research.

My work has been recognized with several awards. Most recently, I received the
Excellent Paper Award at APIEMS 2025 for the research "Fine-Tuning Large Language
Models for Algorithm Selection," and a Gold Prize (2nd place) at the 2024 National
Defense Competition for Graduate Students, jointly hosted by the Korea National
Defense University and KAIST, for "UAV Mission Planning for Clustered Targets with
Deep Reinforcement Learning." At KAIST I was named a Best Counseling Assistant
(2024) and honored with the College of Engineering Leadership Award (Fall 2022) as
one of the top eleven undergraduates for research and leadership. Earlier, I won
several prizes in the Korea Institute of Industrial Engineers (KIIE) project
competitions — a Bronze Prize (5th place, 2022) for reinforcement-learning-based
operating-room scheduling, a Gold Prize (2nd place, 2021) for visiting-nurse
scheduling, and the Best Prize (1st place) at the KAIST IE Frontier for
visiting-nurse scheduling with deep Q-learning.

I am also a co-inventor on a patent, "Method and Device of Deep Reinforcement
Learning-Based Reconnaissance Drone Path Planning for Clustered Targets" (with
Hyun-Jung Kim, Inguk Choi, Sohyun Jeong, and Ji-Yun Shin), filed as Korea Patent
Application No. 10-2025-0154631 on October 23, 2025 (patent pending).

On the teaching side, I served as a teaching assistant for Scheduling Theory and
Application (IE.50036) at KAIST in Spring 2025, and I gave guest lectures on "Large
Language Models for Scheduling" (May 2025) and "Scheduling and Its Real-World
Applications" (Dec. 2025), both at the invitation of Prof. Hyun-Jung Kim. I also
mentored students at the KAIST Center for Gifted Education (Mar. – Jul. 2022). In
April 2026, I was invited to speak on "AI-Enabled Scheduling for Manufacturing
Systems" at the Denmark–Korea Network on Industrial Green Transition in
Manufacturing, on behalf of Prof. Hyun-Jung Kim.

I contribute to the broader community as the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society
(RAS) Technical Committee Student Representative for the Semiconductor
Manufacturing Automation Cluster (since Mar. 2026). Within my department, I have
served as a Counseling Assistant since March 2023 — as Head Counseling Assistant
since September 2023 — providing campus-life information and peer counseling to
KAIST ISysE students, and I served as a Student Council Representative from March
2020 to February 2022, managing academic and non-academic events and leading the
finance team in 2021–2022.
